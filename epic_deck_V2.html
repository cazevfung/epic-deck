Launch Strategy ($6M): Zero Build + IP / Offline

11 / 17

Launch Play Mode — Zero Build

  • Zero Build as launch lead: Aligns with China's mature shooter market; lowers build-learning barrier; widens new-user entry.
  • Layered content rollout: High-intensity fast-paced combat first; build mode, creative maps, and full UGC ecosystem follow in stages.
  • Avoid day-one complexity overload: Use a clear progression path to retain depth players rather than dumping everything at once.

IP Collabs + Offline Events

  • Tier-1 domestic IP launch collabs: Local anime, film, and TV IPs enter Fortnite — manufactured buzz + monetization hooks.
  • Offline "shareable moments": Pop-ups, immersive installations, limited-themed venues — extend online heat into participatory, photo-worthy experiences.
  • Content supply & distribution: Reach broader circles; feed short-video, livestream, and social platforms with sustained material to amplify launch-window noise.

Creator Ecosystem — $4,500,000

12 / 17

Streamer & Video Creator Incentives

  • Audience: Livestream hosts, short-video creators, guide and entertainment content creators.
  • Goal: Rapidly build multi-layer content matrix; amplify reach and topic diffusion.
  • Challenge: Support-A-Creator (SAC) programs are relatively unfamiliar to Chinese users — awareness and participation threshold are high.
  • Solution: Combo of content incentives + SAC to gradually establish the SAC program among Chinese creators.

In-Game Mode Creator Incentives

  • Audience: Deeper ecosystem participants — local mode/map creators and professional developers (including TapTap dev resources).
  • Goal: Build China-local creator supply; upgrade from "an officially published game" to "a sustainably growing mode platform".
  • Challenge: Not just short-term exposure — need to establish UEFN creation habits and sustainable content + monetization paths.
  • Solution: Combine Creator Economy 2.0 and In-Experience Monetization; use UEFN to build shareable modes around local culture and hot topics; partner with TapTap to recruit Chinese game developers into Fortnite content creation.
Esports · $4.5M

China Tournament Infrastructure — $4,500,000

13 / 17

Tournaments serve more than elite players — they help Fortnite China build a "playable, watchable, followable" competitive narrative. This deepens core user stickiness and continuously supplies high-quality content for livestream, video, and social platforms.

Open Tournaments

Open to all players; format, rewards, and ruleset mirror the international version. The goal is to let every player feel and participate in Fortnite's unique competitive atmosphere — and gain something from it.

FNCS China Qualifier (Pro Circuit)

Independent China pro competitive cycle representing Fortnite China's highest tier; serves as the entry point to global FNCS. The platform for Chinese pros to compete on the world stage.

Platform & Streamer Lg

Mid-size tournaments co-hosted with livestream platforms, top streamers, and content hubs — balancing spectacle and shareability. The goal is to raise Fortnite's core-circle discussion heat through content-platform events.
