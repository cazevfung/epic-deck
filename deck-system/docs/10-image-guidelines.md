# 10 Image Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the image rules for the deck system.

Its job is to preserve visual proof and atmosphere without letting image usage become random, unreadable, or impossible to fit.

## Core Principle

Be strict about `image role` and `evidence readability`.

Be flexible about `composition energy` when the page family supports it.

## What This Document Controls

- image role selection,
- composition families,
- aspect-ratio logic,
- caption behavior,
- proof readability,
- image-related fit budgets.

## Image Roles

Every placed image should be assigned one role.

### `hero-spectacle`

Use for:

- cover,
- divider,
- launch anchor,
- campaign-like page,
- emotionally charged chapter transition.

### `hero-proof`

Use for:

- one dominant screenshot or key art that directly carries a strategic point,
- major case-study visual,
- one top image in a stacked proof page.

### `support-proof`

Use for:

- secondary screenshots,
- event photos,
- platform references,
- smaller visual examples that support a lead proof.

### `gallery-proof`

Use for:

- repeated case examples,
- tiled evidence banks,
- validation triptychs,
- multi-example comparison pages.

### `channel-tile`

Use for:

- logo walls,
- partner systems,
- distribution and media coverage proof.

### `diagram-asset`

Use for:

- charts,
- maps,
- flow diagrams,
- framework visuals.

## Composition Families

The original deck proves that image behavior changes by page family. The system should formalize that difference.

### `hero-spectacle-board`

One dominant image field with short integrated copy.

Best for:

- launch pages,
- announcement proof,
- chapter anchor pages.

### `comparison-board`

Two related image or chart fields used to explain a shift or strategic contrast.

Best for:

- old vs. new,
- market shift,
- legacy vs. opportunity.

### `validation-triptych`

Three parallel proof tiles or cards that each hold a short claim, metric, and visual.

Best for:

- validation pages,
- three-signal proof,
- comparable case references.

### `visual-stack`

One hero proof visual plus one secondary support row.

Best for:

- two-column proof pages,
- creator ecosystem pages,
- launch sequence pages.

### `channel-wall`

Grouped logo or platform wall with category logic.

Best for:

- full-channel reach,
- ecosystem breadth,
- partner presence.

## Aspect-Ratio Rules

Preferred defaults:

| Role | Preferred Ratios | Typical Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `hero-spectacle` | `16:9`, `21:9` crop field | cover, anchor, launch |
| `hero-proof` | `16:9`, `4:3` | case hero, main screenshot |
| `support-proof` | `4:3`, `1:1`, `16:9` | support media row |
| `gallery-proof` | `1:1`, `4:3`, `3:4` | repeatable example grid |
| `channel-tile` | `1:1`, flexible wide tile | logo wall |
| `diagram-asset` | source-driven | charts and flow assets |

### Ratio Discipline

- standard pages should avoid more than `2` dominant ratio families,
- mixed-ratio walls are acceptable only when the recipe explicitly supports them,
- do not mix a poster crop, chart crop, and gameplay crop on one small panel row unless each still reads clearly.

## Caption Rules

Use only two standard caption modes.

### Below Caption

Default for:

- proof screenshots,
- charts,
- structured gallery items.

Rules:

- lead with why the image matters,
- keep the caption short,
- use one support line only when it preserves interpretation.

### Overlay Caption

Allowed for:

- hero-spectacle pages,
- launch proof frames,
- strong media tiles,
- logo or gallery slots where external caption space would break fit.

Rules:

- keep overlay copy short,
- never cover the proof target,
- use contrast-safe gradients or plates when needed.

## Evidence Visibility Floor

If an image is proof, the proof must remain readable at slide scale.

### Required

- relevant UI or event moment must remain visible,
- logo identity must remain recognizable,
- supporting captions must stay legible,
- hero proof must not be demoted to decorative background.

### Not Allowed

- tiny screenshots that only work when zoomed,
- stacked proof images that turn into unreadable thumbnails,
- aggressive crops that remove the evidence,
- logo walls that become a blur of tiny badges.

## Image Fit Rules

### Standard Proof Pages

- one dominant proof visual per proof column,
- a second visual may exist only as a support slot,
- do not stack two full-strength hero proofs in one narrow column.

### Validation Pages

- each card may hold one visual,
- visuals should be parallel in scale and prominence,
- one card should not become a mini case-study page.

### Channel Walls

- categories must organize the wall,
- tiles need text fallback when logos are fragile,
- total tile count may be high, but category count should stay manageable.

### Dense Pages

- compress caption length before shrinking the images below readability,
- if proof cannot remain readable, split the page or reduce secondary visuals.

## Preservation Rule

Do not flatten image-led pages into generic neutral cards if the source page's persuasion depends on image atmosphere, event scale, or campaign energy.

If the image is doing narrative work, preserve that work and formalize the pattern.

## Quality Floor

- no visible stretching,
- no blurry hero art,
- no inconsistent crop logic within one family,
- no proof image without interpretive framing,
- no decorative image that pushes out a more important proof asset.

## When To Escalate

Escalate the page if:

- the image cluster needs more than two ratio families without a recipe,
- overlay captions start fighting the image,
- proof is technically present but not readable,
- the page only fits by turning meaningful evidence into decoration.
